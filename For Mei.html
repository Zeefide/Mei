My White Tulip

White tulips rest where my thoughts always go,
Soft ivory whispers in moonlight’s slow glow.
And every petal feels like your name in my chest,
A quiet kind of love that never knows rest.

You move through my silence like spring after snow,
Like white tulips blooming wherever you go.
Even distance can’t dull the light that you are—
You’re soft, yet you stay like a constant bright star.

I catch you in moments the world doesn’t see,
In the calm between breaths, where you’re closest to me.
Like white tulips bending but never once breaking,
That’s how my heart feels when I’m quietly aching.

If love had a color, it would choose you somehow—
A garden of white tulips here and right now.
And I don’t need forever to know what is true:
Even in silence, I am still loving you.
